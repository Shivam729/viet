Saturday, 6 June
Trang An · Mua Cave · Cycling
Ninh Binh · Trang An Valley Homestay (scooters on site) · S$27.50/pax
▾
07:45
Trang An UNESCO Boat Circuit
Arrive at dock by 7:45. 3-hour row through 9 caves and limestone gorges. The most scenic boat ride in Ninh Binh — more dramatic than Tam Coc.
~S$7 Arrive early — queues from 8:30am
11:30
Mua Cave — 500 steps to summit
10-min drive from Trang An. Steep but short — 20-30 min up. Best panoramic view of Ninh Binh's karst landscape anywhere. Do it before lunch while energy is high.
~S$4
13:00
Lunch — Goat meat + com chay
De nui (mountain goat) and com chay (crispy scorched rice) — the two Ninh Binh specialties. Any restaurant on the Tam Coc strip. Ask for muoi cheo on the side.
~S$10
14:30
Countryside scooters / bikes
Hire from guesthouse (~30k VND/hr bikes, ~150k/day scooter). Flat roads through rice paddies. Ride 3km to Bich Dong pagoda — cave temple carved into a cliff. Return via river road.
~S$4
18:00
Sunset riverside walk + bia hoi
Walk the Hoang Long river. Street food dinner + local draught beer at 10k VND a glass.
~S$8

Hire scooters (~150k/day per bike, split riding 2-up = ~S$4/head). Ride through Ban Hieu village, Ban Pom stilt house village, rice terrace viewpoints. Pu Luong is completely off the tourist trail — roads almost entirely to yourselves.

Usually included in homestay price. Eat with the host family. Rice wine is part of the deal — you will be expected to participate.
Included / ~S$7

Tuesday, 9 June
Hanoi — Iconic Spots + Shopping
Hanoi Old Quarter (8 Pax)
▾
09:00
Breakfast — banh cuon street cart
Steamed rice rolls with minced pork. Classic Hanoi morning. ~30–40k VND from a street cart.
~S$2
10:00
Hoan Kiem Lake + Ngoc Son Temple
Walk the lake perimeter (20 min). Cross the red bridge to the island temple. Good first look at the city.
~S$1.50
11:00
Train Street — grab a trackside cafe seat
Hang Bong alley. Cafes line an active railway. Train passes ~3:30pm. Arrive early to guarantee a table with a track view. Sit with egg coffee and wait.
~S$3 Arrive by 11am for afternoon train
12:00
Tam Vi Restaurant — lunch
Specialty banh cuon sit-down restaurant. Renowned for handmade steamed rolls. Book or arrive early for 8.
~S$8
14:00
Dong Xuan Market + Old Quarter shopping
Dong Xuan: covered market with cheap clothing, souvenirs, spices. Old Quarter streets: Hang Bac (silver jewellery), Hang Gai (silk and fabric), Hang Quat (fans and lacquerware). First quoted price is 2x the real price — walk away once.
Budget: ~S$30–50
16:30
Egg coffee — Cafe Giang
39 Nguyen Huu Huan. Tiny hidden alley cafe. Inventor of ca phe trung — egg yolk, condensed milk, and strong Vietnamese robusta. Non-negotiable.
~S$1.70
19:30
Dinner — Bun Cha Huong Lien (Obama spot)
Le Van Huu St. The exact restaurant where Obama ate with Anthony Bourdain in 2016. The table is preserved. Order: bun cha + nem cua be (crab spring rolls). Book ahead or arrive early for 8 people.
~S$3 Small restaurant — call ahead
21:00
Ta Hien Street — Bia Hoi Corner (HANOI PARTY NIGHT)
This is your proper Hanoi night out — the Cat Ba minibus picks up at 09:00 tomorrow so you have the whole night. Plastic stools at the Ta Hien intersection. Freshly brewed draught beer at 7,000 VND a glass. The entire Old Quarter is packed every night.
~S$8 drinks Pack your Ha Long bag BEFORE going out — swimwear, cash, change of clothes
22:30
Bar hop — Old Quarter
Dragonfly (39 Hang Buom), Polite Pub (5 Bao Khanh), Nola (89 Ma May). All within 10-min walk. Live music at some venues. Bars run until 2–3am.
~S$10–15
Late
Late night banh mi — mandatory
Street carts near Hoan Kiem run until 3am. Best way to end a night in Hanoi. Be back and up by 08:00 tomorrow for the Ha Long minibus.
~S$1.50

Ha Long Bay — Cat Ba Ventures Day 1
Hanoi → Lan Ha Bay · Full luggage on board the boat (8 Pax)
▾
08:00
Pack overnight bag — cash only on the bay
Swimwear, change of clothes, sunscreen, and CASH. No ATMs on the bay or boat. Withdraw before leaving Hanoi. Main luggage stays at Airbnb.
Minimum 1,500,000 VND cash
09:00
Cat Ba Ventures minibus pickup
Arranged by Cat Ba Ventures from Hanoi Old Quarter. ~3.5 hrs including ferry crossing.
Included in cruise
11:30
Board boat — depart into Lan Ha Bay
Cat Ba Ventures cruise. US$142/pax (~S$192). All meals, kayaks, gear, guide included. Cabin rooms assigned on board.
S$192 (total cruise)
14:00
Kayaking — rock arches to hidden lagoons
2+ hrs guided kayaking through narrow limestone passages. Bat Cave, Dark Cave. Only accessible by kayak.
16:30
Swim at deserted beach
Boat anchors at a beach accessible only by water. No other tourists.
17:30
Sunset on deck + fishing
Rods and bait provided. Drinks available on board at cost. Watch the sun drop behind the karsts.
19:00
Dinner on deck + squid fishing at night
Full dinner. After dark: squid fishing off the stern with lights — usually a few caught per group. One of the best nights of the trip.

Thursday, 11 June
Cruise Day 2 · Cat Ba afternoon · Overnight bus to Sapa
Lan Ha Bay → Cat Ba → Overnight bus → Sapa (8 Pax)
▾
07:00
Sunrise on deck + breakfast
Wake up to karst islands in every direction. Breakfast on board, included.
09:00
Dau Be Island — kayak hidden lagoons
Ho Ba Ham — 3 interconnected lagoons accessible only at certain tides. Most remote and spectacular part of the bay.
11:00
Final swim + pack cabins
Last swim. Checkout at 11am. Lunch on board, then return to Cat Ba harbour by 12pm.
12:00
Return to Cat Ba harbour — lunch on board
Final meal on the boat. Deboard at Cat Ba pier by 12:00. Full bags come off the boat with you — no Hanoi detour needed. Drop bags at a cafe or the bus company office in Cat Ba town (they hold luggage before departure).
12:30
Cat Ba afternoon — activities while bags are stored
9 hours to fill before the 21:30 bus. Options: Cat Ba National Park hike to Ngu Lam peak (2–3 hrs, great views), Hospital Cave (wartime tunnel network, ~1 hr, very interesting), Cat Ba town beach, or simply eat, rest and explore the island town. No agenda — just enjoy the last few hours of the coast.
Activities ~S$5–10 Hospital Cave entry ~S$3 · Cat Ba National Park entry ~S$4 · both worth it
19:00
Dinner in Cat Ba town + collect bags
Last seafood dinner before the mountains. Cat Ba has great grilled seafood restaurants along the harbour. Collect bags from storage after dinner, head to bus pickup point.
~S$12
21:30
Overnight sleeper bus: Cat Ba → Sapa
Direct or via Hanoi transfer — ~9–11 hrs. VIP cabin sleeper bus: private pod-style berths, AC, comfortable for the whole group. Saves a night's accommodation. Arrive Sapa at a proper check-in time (around 6–8am). Book via baolau.com or catbaexpress.com for the Hanoi–Sapa leg, or Cat Ba Discovery for the direct service.
~S$27–34/pax Last hour Lao Cai→Sapa is winding mountain road — motion sickness tablets recommended

Saturday, 13 June
FANSIPAN SUMMIT — 3,147m
Sapa — Roof of Indochina (8 Pax)
▾
05:00
Wake up — eat something small
Altitude reduces appetite but you need fuel. Bananas and bread at minimum.
05:30
Meet guide at Sapa town square
Licensed guide (~300k VND, S$70 as per your rate). Car to Tram Ton Pass (2,000m) — 25 min drive. Trek begins 6:30am.
~S$70 (guide) Hiking boots, poles, warm layer, gloves
06:30
Begin ascent — Tram Ton Route
12km, 1,147m elevation gain. Through bamboo forest, old-growth pine, alpine meadow. Pace yourself — thin air above 2,500m hits harder than expected.
09:30
Rest stop at 2,800m
Guide provides hot tea and snacks. 20-min rest. Views open dramatically above the treeline. Final 350m is the steepest.
11:30
SUMMIT — Fansipan 3,147m
Roof of Indochina. Summit cross + Buddha statue. On clear days: 360° views across Vietnam, Laos, and the Chinese border. The group photo of the entire trip.
Windproof layer essential — 8–15°C at top
13:30
Cable car descent
Buy at summit kiosk (~820k VND). Cash only. 20-min descent through clouds. Saves 4 hours of hard descent on destroyed legs.
~S$40 (cable car)
15:00
Back in Sapa — rest + shower
Optional foot massage ~150k VND. Your legs will make you regret skipping it.
Massage: ~S$7
19:00
Dinner + night market
Salmon, thang co, black bean wine. You earned it.
~S$15

Sunday, 14 June
Glass Bridge + Thrills → Farewell Dinner
Sapa → Overnight Bus (8 Pax)
▾
07:30
Breakfast + taxi to Glass Bridge (Rong May)
40-min taxi from Sapa town to the Glass Bridge complex on O Quy Ho Pass (~2,200m). Go early — views are clearest before 10am and it's less crowded.
~S$5 (taxi share)
09:00
Glass Bridge — 60m transparent walkway at 2,200m
Vietnam's most dramatic viewpoint. Walk 60m on glass above a deep valley with views of Fansipan — the mountain you just summited yesterday. The glass elevator rises 300m for panoramic views. Entry ~150k VND.
~S$7 entry The day after Fansipan — looking back at the summit you stood on is a special moment
10:00
Add-on thrills — death swing + cable bike
The death swing is the crowd favourite — a freefall swing over the gorge. Cable bike = zip across the valley on a bike frame. Each add-on ~150–200k VND. Pick what the group wants.
~S$7–10 per activity
12:00
Silver Falls stop on the way back
On the road between Glass Bridge and Sapa town. 100m cascade, 5-min walk from the road. Quick stop, great photos. ~70k entry.
~S$3
13:30
Lunch back in Sapa town
Last Sapa lunch. Market area restaurants. Try the Sapa salmon one more time if you didn't get enough yesterday.
~S$8
15:00
Final shopping + pack everything
Buy remaining gifts — indigo scarves, embroidered pouches, silver jewellery. Much cheaper than Hanoi Old Quarter. Pack fully and check out of guesthouse. Store bags until dinner.
18:00
FAREWELL DINNER — full group
Sapa grilled salmon, hotpot, black bean wine, beers. Book ahead for 8. Last proper sit-down meal of the entire trip.
~S$22
20:00
Last night market stroll
Any final items. Collect bags from guesthouse.
22:00
Overnight sleeper bus: Sapa → Hanoi
~350k VND. Arrives Hanoi ~4am on 15th. Go directly to Noi Bai Airport by Grab — do not return to Airbnb.
~S$17 CRITICAL — must catch this for 09:35 flight

TIPS + WARNINGS
09:35 flight on the 15th is unforgiving
You must be at Noi Bai by 06:30. The overnight bus from Sapa leaves at 22:00 on the 14th and arrives ~4am. There is no alternative. Do not miss this bus.
No ATMs on Ha Long Bay or at Fansipan summit
Cable car at the summit costs ~820k VND and is cash only. Withdraw before Sapa. On Ha Long Bay, boat drinks are cash only — withdraw before boarding.
Do not attempt Fansipan in sneakers
Proper hiking boots or trail runners only. The trail is wet, rooted, and steep. Sneakers will ruin your feet and your day. Break in boots before departure.
June is rainy season — plan around it
Afternoon storms are common across northern Vietnam in June. Mornings are usually clear. Schedule outdoor activities and treks for AM. Bring a poncho — umbrellas are useless on scooters.
Fansipan altitude is real
You depart from 2,000m and ascend to 3,147m. Thin air hits hard above 2,500m — headaches, fatigue, and nausea are common. Diamox (start 24hrs before) and electrolytes help significantly. Pace slowly.
Grab not available in Ninh Binh or Pu Luong
Grab works in Hanoi, Sapa town, and Cat Ba area. In Ninh Binh and Pu Luong you'll need local taxis (negotiate price upfront) or scooter hire. Ask your guesthouse to arrange.
Bargaining rules in Vietnamese markets
First quoted price is always 2x the real price. Counter at 40% of asking, settle around 50–60%. Walk away if they don't budge — they will call you back. Never show excitement before agreeing a price.
Best exchange rates in Hanoi
Hang Bac Street money changers in the Old Quarter offer significantly better rates than airport kiosks or hotels. Exchange your SGD there after arriving from Ninh Binh on Day 4.
Vietnamese food etiquette
At homestays and village lunches, accept everything offered — refusing food is impolite. Rice wine will be poured at Pu Luong. A small sip is enough if you don't want more. Tip your guides and village hosts directly in cash.
Sapa shopping — buy here, not Hanoi
Indigo-dyed scarves, embroidered pouches, and silver jewellery are made by the Hmong and Red Dao communities and are cheapest at the Sapa Love Market. The same items cost 2–3x more in Hanoi's Old Quarter.
What to eat where
Ninh Binh: goat meat (de nui) and com chay. Hanoi: bun cha, egg coffee, cha ca la vong. Ha Long: fresh seafood on the boat. Sapa: grilled salmon, thang co, black bean wine. Don't miss these — they're regional and you won't find them elsewhere.
Pack light for the transit days
You sleep on buses on Day 1 (to Ninh Binh), Day 7 (Cat Ba→Sapa overnight), and Day 10 (Sapa→Hanoi). The luxury Cat Ba Ventures cruise takes your full luggage, so no need to leave anything in Hanoi — check out the Airbnb on Day 6 morning and bring everything. After the cruise, store bags at Cat Ba town until the 21:30 bus. Pack a warm jacket near the top of your bag — Sapa is significantly colder than Ha Long Bay.
